Get to know Verdana!

· · ·

Created in 1996, Verdana is a sans-serif font made by Matthew Carter. Verdana features a large x-height Wide proportions & loose letter spacing. Keep strokes legible with wide apertures and counters and a thicker bold weight than other print-ready fonts! This font is specifically designed to be readable at small sizes, so feel free to shrink it down!

· · ·

Did you Know?

· · ·

Verdana's name is based on verdant (meaning something green), and Ana (Howlett's eldest daughter).

Meet Matthew Carter

· · ·

Born October 1, 1937, Carter is a well-known British type designer. He is best known for his fonts like Georgia, Tahoma, Bell Centennial, Miller, Galliard, and of course - Verdana. However, he is credited to having created over 35 typefaces. Carter has won many awards for his contributions to the type world, such as an honoris causa , Doctorate of Humane Letters, a 1995 AIGA medal, a TDC medal in 1997, and a SOTA Typography Award in 2005.
